Why Is Cybersecurity Important?

Protecting your personal information and maintaining good cybersecurity practices is important in preventing customer account takeovers. This happens when a criminal steals account information with the intention to gain unauthorized access to personal accounts and information. Often times, criminals will use the information to create a new fake account benefiting the fraudster.

How Do Criminals Steal Your Info?

Criminals rely on many different techniques to try and steal data. The most common method relies on deception and an effort to have you yourself reveal personal information directly to the criminal. Below are some common techniques that criminals use to try and steal your personal information.

  • Phishing Emails: An email which appears to come form a legitimate source but damands personal information, which otherwise would not be shared over email.
  • Impersonations: Criminals will pretend to work for or represent a legitimate institution and ask for personal information online or over the phone.
  • Stolen Credentials: If personal information is leaked or stolen, criminals can buy this information on the "dark web".
  • Spyware: Some criminals will attempt to install malicious software through texts, emails, and free links. These softwares will then record keystrokes to steal personal information.
  • Dumpster Diving: An old-fashioned method in which criminals will attempt to recover personal information from incorrectly shredded documents.

Signs That Your Information Has Been Compromised:

  • Unfamiliar or unathorized transactions. Money movement, deposits and withdrawls.
  • Missing funds or securities.
  • Incorrect or unauthorized updates to an account. Such as changes in address, phone number, or email.
  • Missing documentation. Such as account statements, reciepts, confirmations, and other finanalcial documents.
  • Unfamiliar accounts or creditors on credit reports.

How You Can Protect Your Information:

Because financial institutions are obligated to protect your information, and provide robust defense measures against cyber threats, criminals often find success by targetting individual people rather than large institutions. Maintaining good cybersecurity practices (like the tips listed below) are an effective way to keep yourself, your information, and your accounts safe from crminals.

  • Use security software that automatically updates: Criminals are always searching for new ways to steal personal information, often times taking advantage of outdated software and protection. Using a security software which automatically updates will ensure your information is kept safe using the latest and best techniques.
  • Treat personal information like cash: Before giving out your personal information, so your research on who will be receiving it, and what they intend to do with it. Do not assume that all individuals and institutions are trustworthy.
  • Make sure your connection in encrypted: When giving out personal information online through a website, check to see whether the web address begins with “https” or look for a lock icon next to the web address. This indicates your connection is encrypted and lowers the chance of a criminal stealing your information.
  • Protect your passwords: When creating an account or a login for your personal information make sure you create a strong password. Some tips to create a robust password are to make it long, mix in letters, numbers, and special characters, never share passwords between different accounts, and do not include your personal details in your passwords.
